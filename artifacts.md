# Artifact Reference

## Artifact Definitions, Formats, and Quality Gates

---

## Artifact Quality Gates (enforced before every artifact output)

- [ ] Required fields complete (per module's `required` field list)
- [ ] Conflict-neutral check: no accusatory language, no opinions stated as fact
- [ ] Trauma-informed check: no shaming, blaming, or re-traumatizing language
- [ ] PII handling decision made: redact / retain / restrict
- [ ] Safety gate completed if trigger level ≥ Yellow
- [ ] Language mode matches role
- [ ] Educational disclaimer appended for legal or clinical content
- [ ] No fabricated statutes, case citations, or clinical standards

`draft: true` flag bypasses gates for working drafts. Never permitted on final shared artifacts.

## Artifact Catalog

### A-01 — Conflict Intake Summary
**Produced by:** MOD-05  
**Roles:** All  
**Format:** Structured narrative with sections  
**Sections:**
- Date / time of intake
- Parties involved (placeholder names by default)
- Presenting conflict (neutral description)
- History and context
- Current impact (emotional, practical, relational)
- Safety notes (if any)
- Requested outcome
- Recommended next module(s)

**Quality gates:** All standard. Neutrality gate is strict.

---

### A-02 — Rewritten Message (3 versions)
**Produced by:** MOD-01  
**Roles:** All  
**Format:** Three labeled options  
**Labels:**
- **Version 1 — Firm:** Clear, direct, boundaried. Still calm.
- **Version 2 — Collaborative:** Opens dialogue. Invites response.
- **Version 3 — Minimal:** Shortest version that communicates the need.

**Quality gates:** Neutrality gate. No sarcasm, no accusation, no inflammatory language in any version.

---

### A-03 — Court-Neutral Co-Parenting Message
**Produced by:** MOD-04  
**Roles:** PAR, ATT, GAL  
**Format:** Single rewritten message + neutrality score (1–10)  
**Neutrality scoring:**
- 9–10: Court-ready
- 7–8: Minor edits suggested
- 5–6: Significant revision needed
- Below 5: Recommend starting over

**Quality gates:** Child-centered check. PII check. Neutrality gate. All parent names become [Parent A] / [Parent B] in default output.

---

### A-04 — Mediation Prep Sheet
**Produced by:** MOD-09  
**Roles:** MED, ARB, ATT, IND, PAR  
**Format:** Structured prep document  
**Sections:**
- Parties and roles
- Session goals (your perspective)
- Core interests (what you really need)
- Positions to avoid leading with
- BATNA (best alternative if no agreement)
- Non-negotiables
- Possible concessions
- Opening statement draft (optional)
- Questions to ask

### A-05 — Peace Agreement Draft
**Produced by:** MOD-10  
**Roles:** MED, ARB, IND, PAR, ORG, NCM  
**Format:** Formal agreement structure  
**Sections:**
- Parties
- Date and context
- Background (neutral, brief)
- Terms agreed to (numbered)
- Duration and review date
- Breach protocol
- Signatures (placeholder)

**Legal disclaimer:** *This agreement is not a legally binding contract unless reviewed and executed with appropriate legal counsel. For binding agreements, consult a licensed attorney.*

### A-07 — Safety Assessment
**Produced by:** MOD-07  
**Roles:** All (mandatory in Orange/Red sessions)  
**Format:** Assessment + resource list  
**Sections:**
- Current safety level (self-assessed)
- Risk factors present (checklist)
- Protective factors present (checklist)
- Immediate safety steps
- Crisis resources (always included)
- Recommended follow-up

**Quality gates:** Safety gate must be completed. Crisis resources mandatory in output.

### A-08 — Safety Plan
**Produced by:** MOD-14  
**Roles:** THR, PSY, SWK, VAD, IND, PAR  
**Format:** Clinical-style safety plan (plain language version for IND/PAR)  
**Sections:**
- Warning signs (personal)
- Internal coping strategies
- People and settings that provide distraction
- People I can ask for help
- Professionals and agencies I can contact
- Making the environment safe
- Reasons for living / reasons to stay safe

**Quality gates:** Safety gate required. Professional review note appended for THR/PSY use.
